Who are you?

I'm a UX designer with a background in chemical and systems engineering. After spending some time in the biotech industry, I feel there’s a huge disconnect between company and customer. I want to try and close that gap by creating products with the user in mind.

I first noticed this gap when working in pharmaceuticals. Management would often frame my work as a global benefit, helping patients over the world with their illnesses. Although technically true, my involvement and contributions were so far upstream in the developmental process, I felt disjointed from the cause.

Engineering and the hard sciences, such as chemistry and biology, take a physical approach to solving the world’s problems. Applying the scientific method and relying on quantifiable data and mathematical models to prove or disprove their hypotheses. After testing the waters of both the pharmaceutical and medical device industries, I gravitated towards a career in design as a way to impact and inspire on an emotional level, not just a physical one – to create experiences based on observations and research from everyday life.


What do you do outside of design?

Outside of design, I love to boulder. You can find me at Vertical Hold in Old Town or taking weekend trips to Palm Springs Tramway or Bishop, CA to climb outdoors. I try and make use of my U.S. National Parks pass, so impromptu road trips aren't out of the question. Other than that, staying home and watching Netflix is my usual go-to. I also enjoy attending seminars and workshops with AIGA, SDXD and UX Speakeasy as well.
